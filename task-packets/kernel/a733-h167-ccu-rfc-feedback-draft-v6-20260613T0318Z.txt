Hi Junhui,

Thanks for the A733 CCU series. I have been testing it on a Radxa Cubie
A7S (A733 / SUN60IW2) as part of mainline board bring-up, and I have
some hardware results that look relevant to this patch and the A733 CCU
driver.

The short version is:

- `ahb-cpus` needs to stay enabled, otherwise later R-CCU/RTC register
  accesses can stall after clk_disable_unused().
- The storage/NSI fabric clocks also need special handling for SDMMC0
  normal IDMA to reach memory.
- The NSI clock uses `CCU_FEATURE_UPDATE_BIT`; committing the
  boot-programmed NSI mux/divider state during probe was required in my
  tests, together with keeping the NSI fabric clocks enabled.

All of this was tested with mainline plus the A733 CCU/RTC/pinctrl
series on a Cubie A7S over UART. I have not sent patches because I would
like maintainer guidance on the preferred modelling.

1) `ahb-cpus` at 0x5c0 BIT(28)

The CPU appears to reach the R-CCU and RTC register windows through the
AHB bridge controlled by `ahb-cpus`. Since the bridge has no ordinary
Linux device consumer, clk_disable_unused() can gate it during the
unused-clock walk. Later R-domain register reads then stall the bus.

With only this gate marked critical, the unused-clock walk completed on
the Cubie A7S and later R-CCU/RTC register accesses remained live. This
also disproved my earlier suspicion that `ext-osc32k-gate` itself was an
absent register; it reads normally once the CPUS bridge remains enabled.
The remaining RTC issue on this board is just that `ext-osc32k` has no
DT source and therefore appears as an orphan parent.

2) storage/MBUS/NSI fabric clocks

After the `ahb-cpus` issue was fixed, SDMMC0 still exposed two separate
fabric symptoms:

- with the store lane disabled by the unused-clock walk, SDMMC0 hit
  repeated `fatal err update clk timeout`;
- with the store lane kept alive but NSI still disabled, the host could
  initialize but normal IDMA still stopped at `Waiting for root device`.

The diagnostic shape that reached root kept these clocks critical:

- `ahb-store`
- `mbus-store`
- `mbus-msi-lite0`
- `nsi`
- `bus-nsi`

That shape, combined with the NSI update commit described below, allowed
SDMMC0 normal IDMA to enumerate the card, discover `mmcblk0` partitions,
mount the root filesystem read-only, and start `/bin/sh`.

Important caveat: `mbus-msi-lite0` was part of the verified
storage-fabric bundle, but I have not yet isolated it from `ahb-store`
and `mbus-store`. I have a narrower no-`mbus-msi-lite0` build prepared
and statically validated, but I have not run the hardware proof yet. So
for now I can only say it was part of the verified bundle, not that it
has been independently proven required.

3) NSI update bit at 0x580 BIT(27)

The diagnostic that first made SDMMC0 normal IDMA progress pointed at
absolute address 0x02002580 bit 27. In the CCU driver, that maps to the
A733 NSI clock register at offset 0x580 and `CCU_SUNXI_UPDATE_BIT`.
The unchanged readback after writing this bit is expected, since the bit
self-clears after committing the update.

The NSI clock already has `CCU_FEATURE_UPDATE_BIT`, but in the boot path
I tested, no consumer commits the boot-programmed NSI state before
SDMMC0 IDMA needs the fabric. The parent/gate/div helper paths can OR in
`CCU_SUNXI_UPDATE_BIT` when `CCU_FEATURE_UPDATE_BIT` is set, but the MP
rate path itself does not appear to OR in the update bit, and no normal
consumer path exercised a parent/gate transition early enough here.

In my tests, pulsing the update bit once during the A733 CCU probe was
necessary but not sufficient: the pulse alone still stopped at
`Waiting for root device`, while the pulse plus critical `nsi` and
`bus-nsi` clocks let SDMMC0 normal IDMA reach root.

The local probe-time experiment was effectively:

        val = readl(reg + SUN60I_A733_NSI_REG);
        writel(val | CCU_SUNXI_UPDATE_BIT, reg + SUN60I_A733_NSI_REG);

with `SUN60I_A733_NSI_REG` defined as 0x580.

I am not sure whether you would prefer this as an A733-specific probe
fixup or whether sunxi-ng should commit boot-programmed state for
`CCU_FEATURE_UPDATE_BIT` MP clocks more generally during registration. I
am also not sure whether the storage/NSI fabric dependencies should be
represented as critical clocks or modelled with explicit consumers.

If useful, I can share:

- UART logs for the `ahb-cpus` hang and the fixed unused-clock walk;
- the storage-fabric-only result that clears the SDMMC clock-update
  timeout but still does not reach root;
- the NSI-update-only result that still does not reach root;
- the NSI update plus critical NSI/storage fabric result that reaches
  `mmcblk0`, mounts root read-only, and starts `/bin/sh`;
- the split local draft patches used for the tests.

Tested-on: Radxa Cubie A7S (A733 / SUN60IW2), mainline plus this A733
CCU series and the related A733 RTC and pinctrl series, UART console,
aarch64 GCC build.
