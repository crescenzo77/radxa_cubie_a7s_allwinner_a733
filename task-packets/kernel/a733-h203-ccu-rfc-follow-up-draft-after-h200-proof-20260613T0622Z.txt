Hi Junhui,

Following up with a more concrete result from the Cubie A7S testing.

I now have a cleaned four-patch local stack for the CCU/NSI part of the
issue, and I boot-tested the exact cleaned commit on Radxa Cubie A7S. The
kernel reached:

- clk_disable_unused()
- genpd unused-domain disable
- SDMMC0 host initialization
- mmcblk0 partition discovery
- read-only ext4 root mount
- /bin/sh as init

The tested commit was:

    de486cb24c36 ("clk: sunxi-ng: a733: keep GIC and CPU peri clocks critical")

on top of the local A733 bring-up base, with the relevant CCU changes split as:

1. keep `ahb-cpus` critical, because later R-CCU/RTC register accesses appear
   to depend on the CPUS/R-domain AHB bridge remaining live;
2. keep the storage/NSI fabric bundle critical:
   `ahb-store`, `mbus-store`, `mbus-msi-lite0`, `nsi`, and `bus-nsi`;
3. pulse the A733 NSI clock update bit at CCU offset 0x580 during CCU probe so
   the boot-programmed NSI mux/divider state is committed before SDMMC0 IDMA
   needs the fabric;
4. keep `gic` and `cpu-peri` critical.

The narrowing tests were useful:

- dropping `ahb-iommu0-sys`, `ahb-iommu1-sys`, and `pll-periph0-480M` still
  passed;
- dropping both `gic` and `cpu-peri` failed after clk_disable_unused();
- keeping only `gic` or only `cpu-peri` also failed before root;
- the final shape above passed through root mount and /bin/sh.

The generated patches pass checkpatch --strict, trailer hygiene, and a focused
public-hygiene scan. I have not sent them as a standalone series because they
modify clocks introduced by this in-flight A733 CCU RFC, and I would rather
align with the preferred model first.

Would you prefer me to send the cleaned local stack as an RFC/RFT reply against
this CCU series, or would you prefer to fold any of this into the next revision
of the A733 CCU support?

I can also share the UART logs and exact patch text if that is useful.
